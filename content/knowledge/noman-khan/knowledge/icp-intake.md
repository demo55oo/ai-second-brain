## Core Service & Outcome

### The Main Offer or Service (one line)
I work with technology and cybersecurity companies to scale their post-sales and customer success organizations globally.

### The Biggest Outcome or Transformation Clients Achieve
What I love seeing is when a company realizes that customer success isn't just about support or renewals — it's their most powerful lever for sustainable growth.

I help them design systems, teams, and data models that drive measurable outcomes:

- Higher net retention
- Stronger partner ecosystems
- Faster time to value for their customers

The shift is cultural as much as operational: **customer success becomes part of their go-to-market DNA.**

### Who Benefits Most (Ideal Company or Buyer Type)
Most likely the **CRO**, the **Chief Customer Officer**, or the **Customer Success Leader** would benefit from the services.

## Target ICP Insights

### What problems do your clients want solved most urgently?
Most SaaS companies want to fix the parts of their customer lifecycle that are leaking revenue, slowing down product adoption, and putting renewals at risk. They face several challenges:

- Stop churn and stabilize retention fast
- Improve adoption and time-to-value immediately
- Build a scalable operating model that aligns product, sales, and post-sales

### What frustrates them about current solutions or competitors?
Most customers get frustrated because current solutions in the market only treat symptoms, not systems. It is really important to understand the root cause first and then provide a scalable, revenue-producing customer operating model that can deliver results.

### What do they desire most (results, feelings, recognition)?
Since I have been a customer for a CS solution, I typically look for measurable results, a sense of control on what will be deployed, and the recognition that comes from building a customer engine that actually performs. While the ultimate goal is to fix the leaky bucket, at the end you also want to elevate the customer experience.

### What makes your clients feel at risk if they delay action or choose wrong?
This is the opposite of what was just discussed above. The biggest issue or risk is regret of no action — or choosing the wrong solution. This can have a direct negative impact on revenue, customer trust, and also create some political noise for the leaders themselves.

> *Risk perception + urgency triggers*
