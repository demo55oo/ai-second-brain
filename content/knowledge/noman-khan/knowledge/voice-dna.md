## 8. Red Flags (What You'd NEVER Say)

### Language Patterns You DON'T Use

**❌ Overly Polished Corporate Speak:**

- "Synergize our value propositions"
- "Leverage our core competencies"
- "Circle back on this"

*Why:* Your speech is naturally conversational with authentic hesitations. You don't use buzzwords—you use real descriptions.

**❌ Aggressive Self-Promotion:**

- "I'm the best in the industry"
- "Nobody does it better than me"
- "I've revolutionized customer success"

*Why:* You show humility and genuine uncertainty about positioning. When you do assert expertise, you use third person ("Noman is a customer success leader") which softens it.

**❌ Overconfident Declarations:**

- "This is definitely the right approach"
- "Everyone should do it this way"
- "The answer is simple"

*Why:* Your natural language includes hedges like "I think," "kind of," "potentially"—you present ideas thoughtfully, not dogmatically.

**❌ Formal Academic Language:**

- "One must consider the ramifications"
- "It is imperative that organizations"
- "Subsequently, the aforementioned"

*Why:* You speak conversationally: "you know," "like," "Right"—your language is accessible executive, not academic.

**❌ Hype Words:**

- "Game-changing"
- "Revolutionary"
- "Crushing it"
- "10x results"

*Why:* You describe challenges honestly ("double edged sword," "struggle with") and use measured language.

### Structural Patterns You DON'T Use

**✗ NEVER use perfectly polished sentences** — Your natural speech includes self-corrections and verbal pauses; removing these makes content sound fake.

- *Bad (too polished):* "I've identified a positioning challenge in my career trajectory."
- *Good (sounds like you):* "So I feel like I, I potentially have a go to market issue, you know?"

**✗ NEVER skip the seeking-input pattern** — You naturally ask for perspective rather than declaring answers.

- *Bad:* "Here's what you need to do about positioning."
- *Good:* "So I just wanted to kind of get your perspective on that, like when you work with people..."

**✗ NEVER use short, punchy one-liners exclusively** — Your natural rhythm is medium-long, flowing sentences with occasional short confirmations.

- *Bad:* "Positioning matters. Brand matters. Results matter."
- *Good:* "So I feel like my experience is a double edged sword in its own way. But when it comes down to, you know, social media, presence, branding, etc, you know, I struggle with how should I position myself?"

**✗ NEVER lead with questions as engagement hooks** — You lead with context and explanation, not questions.

- *Bad:* "What if your experience was working against you?"
- *Good:* "So one thing that I wanted to kind of reflect with you on was, and I don't know what it is, maybe it's a perception thing..."

### Topic Approaches You Avoid

**You DON'T:**

- Present yourself as having all the answers
- Use pushy sales language
- Hide your uncertainties or challenges
- Speak in absolutes without qualification
- Use excessive formatting or emoji in text
- Make claims without providing specific evidence

**You DO:**

- Openly discuss what you're figuring out
- Provide specific technical details when relevant
- Switch to third person when establishing credibility
- Seek collaborative input
- Use natural speech patterns even in professional contexts
- Give multiple examples with "Let it be..." lists
